## What Is a Frame Shift?

A frame shift is the moment mid-work when you realize the problem is fundamentally different than you thought. You were building X, solving for Y, architecting around Z — and then it clicked: the *real* issue is something about how the problem itself is constructed.

**Recognition signals:**
- "Wait, this isn't actually about [what I thought]"
- "The real breakthrough here isn't the tool, it's something about *how* [teams/people/systems] think"
- "I realized mid-work that the constraint isn't technical, it's [narrative/structural/philosophical]"

**Not a frame shift:**
- Interesting discovery within the existing frame ("we found a better algorithm")
- Execution refinement ("this works better if we...")
- Problem gotten harder or easier ("this turns out to need more work")

Frame shifts reorient the *problem itself*.
